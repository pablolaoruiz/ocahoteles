Cruceiro do Galo Duerming Apartments are 5 minutes away walking from the Obradoiro Square, very close to the most important places in Santiago. You can get to them without needing any means of transport. 

The Cathedral, along with the four squares surrounding it -Obradoiro, Quintana, Inmaculada and Praterías- and the main historic streets are a must see for first-time visitors, but there are other jewels that no one should leave out of their touristic itinerary. 


VIEWPOINTS

Alameda Viewpoint


One of the best places to admire the most beautiful view of the Cathedral. Here you can also enjoy the sculptures of the great poet Rosalía de Castro, the two colourful Marías, and a small pond with ducks.


Bonaval Park


What used to be an old cemetery is now a lovely park, perfect for photography lovers thanks to its astonishing panoramic view.


MUSEUMS

Within the vast cultural activities in Santiago de Compostela, we highlight certain museums that are a must-visit and where you can find important permanent exhibitions as well as varied collections by well-known national and international artists.

We highlight Centro Galego de Arte Contemporáneo, the Monastery of San Martín Pinario, the Museo do Poblo Galego, the Bonaval Museum, the Museum of the House of Troy and the Museo das Preregriacións, among others.



CIDADE DA CULTURA DE GALICIA

Designed by the american architect Peter Eisenman, it is located on the top of the Gaias Mount, where you can enjoy one of the best views of the city. It was created to display the best Galician and worldwide culture.

Its particular granite buildings linked by streets and squares have an advance technological level and create an space of excellence oriented to Galicia’s future and its international presence.


CUISINE

In Santiago there is a magnificent representation of the Galician restaurant industry and its varied forms: from select restaurants, lovely economic restaurants or inns, pulperías and tempting seafood restaurants, and also parrilladas and churrasquerías, A heritage from the massive Galician emigration to Argentina, or the much frequented jamonerías, vinotecas and taverns where you can also enjoy tapas.
