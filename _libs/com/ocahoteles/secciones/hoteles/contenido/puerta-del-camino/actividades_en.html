Enjoy the Museums of Santiago de Compostela and the Galician gastronomy at the Hotel Oca Puerta del Camino.

Museums

To the vast cultural offer available in Santiago de Compostela, we must add a wide range of museums. It is worth to mention the following ones:


• Cidade da Cultura: designed by Peter Eisenman, its construction began in 1999 and it is composed of 4 buildings located on the Gaiás Hill. 

http://www.cidadedacultura.gal


• Museo do Pobo Galego: founded in 1976 and located on the Monastery of Santo Domingo de Bonaval. Its aim is to investigate, preserve, spread, defend and promote Galician culture.

  http://www.museodopobo.es


• Centro Galego de Arte Contemporánea: it was designed by the portuguese architect Álvaro Siza and it was built between 1988 and 1993. Here you can find exhibitions with different artistic styles. 

www.cgac.org


• Eugenio Granell Foundation Museum: it is a museum created by the painter Eugenio Granell where you can observe both his paintings and the creations of other artists. Here they also organise artistic activities throughout the year. 

www.fundacion-granell.org


• Da Troia House: it is a recreation of the student atmosphere of the well-known hostel managed by Doña Generosa in Santiago de Compostela at the end of the XIX century. It was immortalized thanks to the work of  D. Alejandro Pérez Lugín and his novel “La casa de La Troya”.

www.lacasadelatroya.gal


• Museo Pedagóxico de Galicia: its objective is to recover, protect, study, show and spread all the education policies that will manifest the variety and great variety of the pedagogical heritage in Galicia. 

http://www.edu.xunta.es/mupega/taxonomy/term/17


Cuisine


Galicia is a community well known for the quality of its product, both the ones gotten at the sea and the ones that come from the land. At Oca Puerta del Camino you will be able to enjoy local cuisine but, if what you want is to tapear while visiting the city, we can recommend you the following areas:

• Rúa do Franco: a street in the historical area, very well known for its wines and tapas. It goes from the Alameda until Obradoiro Square.

• Market: here you can buy all kinds of products; both land products from the near vegetables gardens and seafood from the Galician estuary. In the closest streets  you can find nice places to tapear and taste some of these products.

• Rúa de San Pedro / Casas Reais: it is the entrance to the historic area of the French Way, where you can also find restaurants and pubs to begin the night tour. 



Destination: Galicia

Thanks to the ideal location of Santiago, our hotel can be used as a base to get to know the rest of the autonomous community:


• A Coruña: 45 minutes away by car from the Hotel; it is worth to mention the Tower of Hercules, the Square of María Pita or the Riazor and Orzán beaches.


• Rías Baixas: 50 minutes away by car. We must point out towns like Combarro, Sanxenxo, O Grove and its La Toja island, and Cambados. The cuisine of this place is based on the seafood gotten in the estuary and the beaches.


• Vigo- Cíes Islands: 1 hour away from Vigo; it is worth to mention its harbour and A Pedra market, as well as the trip to the Cíes Islands, chosen best beach of the world by The Guardian.


• A Mariña: 1 hour and a half, bordering to Asturias, you can find this coastal area where the famous As Catedrais beach is located.


• Ourense: 1 hour into the inland you can find Ourense, well known for its Baths, especially the ones in A Chavasqueira which are outdoor baths. 


• Ribeira Sacra: 1 hour and a half away, it is where the Miño and Sil rivers converge between mountains covered in vineyards.
