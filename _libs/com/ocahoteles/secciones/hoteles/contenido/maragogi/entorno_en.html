Maragogi, located on the state of Alagoas, northeast region of Brazil, is known as the “Costa dos Corais”. This state is halfway between the cities of Recife and Maceió. It is the second most important touristic city of the state, after the capital (Maceió), and in tourism we find its main source of income. Its beautiful beaches full of coconut trees, an emerald-like blue sea and its coral reefs along with its famous natural swimming pools, create a marvelous region, perfect for tourists who look for rest and disconnect from everyday stress. 


History

Originally, Maragogi was a fishing village known as Gamela, that belonged to the municipality of Porto Calvo, whose main resource was fishing and agriculture, especially the sugar cane. 

The village of Maragogi was very active during the fights against the Dutch during the XVII century. There was heavy fighting in the Praia de Barra Grande and in the Praia de São Bento, during the advance of the invaders from Recife city towards Porto Calvo. Afterwards, during the XIX century, in 1832, also took place the Guerra dos Cabanos, that would last three years. 

In the year 1875 the village became a town and changed its name to “Isabel”, in honour of the princess who freed the African-American people of slavery. A few years later, in 1982, the town became a city and its named changed again to “Maragogi”, after the river that flows by the city and thought to mean “river of the Marauba tribes” according to some historians. 


Tourism in Maragogi


Carnival:

The Maragogi Carnival is the most important festivity in the municipality, just like in all the cities in Brazil. It is celebrated every year and thousands of people visit the city. The dances, the frevo and the parade that take the streets of the city guarantee fun for the tourists that visit the city during any popular festivity. 


Matriz de Santo Antônio Church:

It was built at the end of the XIX century and it is located in the Santo Antonio Square. It is one of the most important churches in Maragogi and a spot of great touristic interest.


Nossa senhora da Guia Church:

It is another monument we can observe during our visit to Maragogi.


São Bento Church:

It is located on a hill sharing the same name and it was built in the XVII century. What remains now are the outer walls of the church, which was once of great importance for the development of the municipality.
